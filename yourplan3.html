IS DEMENTIA A DISEASE OR A SYMPTOM?

Dementia is a general term that desribes a group of symptoms and not a specific disease. Dementia affects thinking, reasoning, and can interfere with a person's ability to care for themselves.

It is not a normal part of aging.

ARE ALZHEIMER'S DISEASE AND DEMENTIA THE SAME THING?

Alzheimer's disease is the most commonly diagnosed form of dementia; however it is not the only one.

WHY DOES DEMENTIA NEED IT'S OWN PLANNING?

As dementia progresses, a person may lose the ability to express their healthcare preferences. It is important to think about what you might want in this situation. If you let your loved ones and healthcare team know your preferences in advance, they won't have to guess, and you can be assured that you will receive the level of care that is most important to you.
